Software Requirements Engineering: Call for project proposals

Summary: We are looking for an industry project for the Software Requirements Engineering (SE754) course at the University of Auckland. Do you have a website or application you want developed or refreshed? Want to see multiple prototypes to give you ideas? Propose a project for SE754! We are looking for one industry project. Students will elicit requirements from you and develop prototypes to demonstrate how their design will meet your requirements. Students will work in teams of 3-4. All teams will work on the same project, so multiple prototypes will be developed for the same project.

Project size: An ideal project should be small enough to be feasible within roughly 8 weeks of the semester, but large enough to be challenging for the student teams. The focus of the project will be on understanding customer requirements and developing a prototype that meets those requirements. A team of 3-4 students will spend around 7-8 hours per person per week on the project.

Your Involvement: You will be the customer for the project. You would need to come to 2-3 one-hour lectures to discuss your project requirements with the students and give feedback on prototype demonstrations.

What's in it for you? This provides an excellent opportunity for you to explore a prototype of a new software application or extend/refresh an existing system. Working with our final year BE and ME studies students will also help raise your visibility as potential employers for our future graduates.

Course Dates: 24th July to 27th Oct (with a mid-semester break from 4-16th Sep)

Class Level: Fourth year Bachelor of Engineering students and Masters of Engineering Studies students

Course lecturers: Dr Kelly Blincoe (k.blincoe@auckland.ac.nz) and Dr Xuyun Zhang (xuyun.zhang@auckland.ac.nz)

Please contact us if you have any queries.

Best regards,

Kelly Blincoe and Xuyun Zhang
